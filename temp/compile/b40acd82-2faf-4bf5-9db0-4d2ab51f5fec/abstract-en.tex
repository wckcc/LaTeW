Aiming at the high-incidence semantic fraud problem in mobile financial scenarios, traditional mobile banking risk control has defects such as insufficient semantic analysis capability, single dimension of user behavior perception, and unauditable AI decision-making. Based on AI Agent and MCP standardized interactive protocol, this paper builds a four-layer HIRD risk control architecture of perception, cognition, governance and execution, constructs a multi-modal risk perception system, and combines retrieval-augmented generation technology to accurately identify transaction intentions. The dynamic weight algorithm converts probabilistic AI reasoning results into deterministic risk control decisions, forming a full-process auditable intelligent risk control closed loop. The system realizes engineering implementation of mobile terminal non-inductive collection and backend asynchronous orchestration, and its performance is verified through multiple groups of comparative and ablation experiments. The test results show that the system risk control decision delay is within 680ms, the semantic fraud identification recall rate reaches 94%, and the false positive rate is less than 1.2%. It can effectively identify implicit social engineering fraud, meet the real-time, security and compliance audit requirements of financial scenarios, and make up for the technical shortcomings of traditional mobile risk control.
